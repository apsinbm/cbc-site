# Heritage & Club Identity

## Historical Foundation

### Founding Era - 1940s
Coral Beach & Tennis Club was established in the 1940s during a pivotal period in Bermuda's development as an international destination. The club was conceived as an exclusive retreat that would embody the finest traditions of Bermudian hospitality while catering to an international membership seeking sophistication and natural beauty.

### Early Vision
The founders envisioned a club that would:
- Preserve Bermuda's natural coastal beauty
- Provide world-class facilities in an intimate setting
- Foster international friendships and cultural exchange
- Maintain the highest standards of service and amenities
- Respect and celebrate authentic Bermudian culture

## Architectural Heritage

### Bermudian Style
CBC's buildings exemplify classic Bermudian architecture with:
- **White Limestone Roofs**: Traditional stepped roofs designed to collect rainwater
- **Pastel Colors**: Soft coral, pink, and cream tones reflecting the island's palette
- **Verandas and Terraces**: Open-air spaces taking advantage of ocean breezes
- **Coral Stone Construction**: Using local materials in traditional methods
- **Hurricane-Resistant Design**: Built to withstand Bermuda's seasonal storms

### Landscape Integration
The club's design philosophy emphasizes harmony with the natural environment:
- Buildings positioned to maximize ocean views
- Native plant landscaping preserving the coastal ecosystem
- Minimal disruption to the natural coastline
- Integration of indoor and outdoor living spaces
